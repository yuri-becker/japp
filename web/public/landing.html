What's Planning Poker?

Planning Poker is a way of estimating tasks. Team members place cards containing their estimate face-down on a table.

When everyone has placed their cards, the cards are flipped - revealing everyone's estimates. If the estimates are different, you discuss. You then repeat this until every estimate is the same.

Why do I need Planning Poker?

Planning Poker somewhat avoids cognitive bias you have with other methods for estimations.

Should I use Planning Poker?

Depending on the nature of your project, estimates can be pointless and a pure waste of time. In other projects, estimates can make sense, or sometimes you just need to assign Story Points so important people can look at important velocity charts.

So if you need estimations, Planning Poker surely is one way of doing them.
